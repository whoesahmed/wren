What WREN Does

Built for the curious.

Every feature exists to get you from question to understanding as fast as possible — with your ears as much as your eyes.

Instant Wikipedia Search

Type any topic and WREN fetches a clean, readable summary directly from Wikipedia's API — no ads, no clutter.

Voice Search

Click the microphone and speak. WREN listens, transcribes, and searches — hands-free knowledge on demand.

Text-to-Speech

Every answer is read aloud automatically. Perfect for multitasking, learning on the go, or accessibility.

Search History

Your last 10 searches are saved locally and persist between sessions. Revisit any topic in one tap.

No Backend, No Data

WREN runs entirely in your browser. No server, no account required, no data ever leaves your device.

Mobile Ready

Fully responsive across all screen sizes. Works as a progressive web app you can install on your home screen.

How It Works

Three steps to any answer.

01
Enter your topic

Type or speak anything — a historical event, a scientific concept, a person, a place. WREN understands natural language.

02
WREN searches Wikipedia

The Wikipedia API is queried in real time. If your exact query doesn't match, WREN finds the closest related articles automatically.

03
Read and hear the answer

A clean summary appears instantly and is read aloud. Click "Read full article" to go deeper on Wikipedia.

What is the Silk Road?
Silk Road
The Silk Road was an ancient network of trade routes that connected the East and West, stretching from China through Central Asia to the Mediterranean. It was central to cultural, commercial and technological exchange between the Han dynasty, Parthian Empire and Roman Empire.
